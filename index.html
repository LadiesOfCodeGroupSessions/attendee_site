When Every Wednesday from 5:30pm - 7:30pm GMT+1

Where Online - Zoom & Slack - Join from anywhere!

Sign up: https://forms.gle/i5vRqGeVqQZAjSpv5 We add new sign-ups at the end of every even month, except December. You'll me notified in a few weeks with your start date.

Would you like to share your skills and experience with the next generation of coders? We are looking for folks with experience in TDD / Ensemble Programming & our current languages: Python, JavaScript or Java.
Join us as a Special Guest: https://forms.gle/7W5pAsGWrWNSBWzb7
How we work Every week, multiple teams come together and work on either katas or applications of their own design.
While doing this, every member works on the code at the same time.
The role of the Driver (the person writing the code) is rotated through-out the session while the rest of the team perform the Navigator role.
We often have discussions before we start coding. Some examples include: Good coding and good testing practices, Functional vs OO programming, & different types of TDD.

Reasons to join You want to practice a new coding language with other women also learning that language.
You'd like to learn Test Driven Development/Design.
You want to dedicate time to katas.
You're keen to try ensemble coding (mobbing).
You want to meet more women developers.
You want to gain experience working in a team (good for self-learners).
You enjoy talking about coding with other people.
How often do I need to attend? We suggest aiming for a ratio of 40 out of 52 weeks.
Having consistent attendance helps the team but life happens.
We understand that you'll need to do other things on Wednesdays.
Also, we recommend that if you have taken leave from work then you should take leave from the group too and enjoy your break.

I know nothing about TDD or Ensemble coding, can I still join? Definitely! We'll start right at the beginning.

What do I need to know before attending? You'll need to know the basics of at least of one programming language before attending - that's it! The basics include: Setting up a project, basic syntax, variables, primitive types, loops and functions
